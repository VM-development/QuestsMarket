Sitting for hours is hard on your body even if you exercise. The fix is simple: once an hour, stand up and walk for a few minutes — a "movement snack." Refill your water, take a call on your feet, or just loop the room.

Why it works. In a Columbia University study (Diaz et al., 2023), breaking up sitting with short walks cut post-meal blood-sugar spikes by up to 58% and lowered blood pressure by 4–5 mmHg — plus less fatigue and better mood. The sweet spot was a 5-minute walk every 30 minutes, but any regular get-up beats sitting straight through.

How to use this quest. It nudges you each hour during the workday — when it does, stand up and move for 2–5 minutes, then check it off. Want the research-grade dose? Add a second time on the half-hour, or shorten the gap. Adjust or remove hours to fit your schedule when you add it.

General wellness tip, not medical advice. Move within a comfortable range.
