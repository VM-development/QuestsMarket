A short daily routine to undo desk-and-couch slouch — three slow, controlled moves for a taller spine and freer shoulders. Move slowly, breathe, and aim for a tall, easy posture rather than forcing range. 8–10 reps each is plenty.

1. Kneeling thoracic opener

Kneel on the floor (or even on the couch). Sit your hips back toward your heels and reach your arms forward and long, letting your chest sink so you feel a gentle stretch and extension through your upper back. Reach a little further on each exhale, then return slowly. Target: thoracic (upper-back) mobility.

2. Wall arm slides

Stand with your back, head, and arms against a wall, elbows bent in a “goalpost” shape. Keeping everything in contact with the wall, slide your arms slowly up overhead and back down. If your arms lift off the wall, shorten the range — quality over height. Target: shoulder mobility.

3. Scapular push-ups

Get on your hands and knees in a tabletop position, keeping a level, neutral body line (the dashed line). With your arms straight, let your chest sink as you squeeze your shoulder blades together, then push the floor away to spread them apart and round your upper back. Only the shoulder blades move. Target: the muscles around the shoulder blades.
